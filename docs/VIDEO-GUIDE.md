# 🎥 Video Guide: Install NodePress in 5 Minutes

> **Use this script to record a video tutorial.**
> Total length: ~5 minutes
> Skill level: Absolute beginner
> Tone: Friendly, encouraging, slow-paced

---

## 📋 Before You Record

**What you need:**

- A computer with NodePress installed (fresh, not yet set up)
- Screen recording software (OBS Studio is free)
- A quiet room
- A friendly, relaxed voice

**What to prepare:**

- Have NodePress downloaded and ready in a folder
- Close all other programs
- Clear your desktop
- Make browser fonts larger (zoom to 125%)

---

## 🎬 Video Script

---

### [0:00] Intro

**On screen:** Camera or animated logo

**Script:**

"Hi there! Welcome to this quick guide.

Today I am going to show you how to install NodePress.

NodePress is a free website builder.

Think of it as a modern, fast CMS.

And here is the best part.

You do NOT need to be a programmer.

If you can double-click a file and answer 5 questions, you can do this.

Let's get started!"

---

### [0:15] Step 1: Download NodePress

**On screen:** Browser showing `https://github.com/superdevids/nodepress`

**Script:**

"First, we need to download NodePress.

Open your browser and go to this address:

`github.com/superdevids/nodepress`

You will see a page like this.

See the green button that says 'Code'?"

**Action:** Move mouse cursor to the green "Code" button

**Script:**

"Click it."

**Action:** Click the button, showing the dropdown

**Script:**

"Now click 'Download ZIP.'

This will download NodePress to your computer."

---

### [0:30] Step 2: Extract and Find the Installer

**On screen:** Desktop or Downloads folder

**Script:**

"Once the download finishes, find the ZIP file.

It will be in your Downloads folder.

Right-click the ZIP file.

Click 'Extract All' on Windows."

**Action:** Demonstrate extracting

**Script:**

"Or just double-click it on a Mac.

Now you have a folder called 'nodepress.'

Open the folder.

Inside, you will see the installer file.

On Windows, it is called `install.bat`.

On Mac, it is called `install.sh`."

**Action:** Point to the file with the mouse

---

### [1:00] Step 3: Double-Click the Installer

**On screen:** Folder with installer file highlighted

**Script:**

"Now for the easiest step.

Double-click the installer."

**Action:** Double-click the installer

**On screen:** Terminal window opening

**Script:**

"A black window will open.

This is normal.

Do NOT close this window.

The installer is working.

It is checking your computer to see what you need."

**Action:** Let the installer run for a moment

**Script:**

"See all that text?

The installer is getting everything ready.

If it asks you to install something, just follow the link.

Install what it says, then run the installer again.

For most people, everything works on the first try."

---

### [1:30] Step 4: Your Browser Opens

**On screen:** Wait for browser to appear, or cut to a pre-recorded shot

**Script:**

"After about a minute, your browser will open."

**Action:** Show the browser opening to the setup page

**Script:**

"If the browser does not open, do not worry.

Just open Chrome, Edge, or Safari yourself.

Type this in the address bar: `localhost:3000`

Press Enter, and you will see the setup page.

Let's set up your website now."

---

### [2:00] Step 5: Answer 5 Questions

**On screen:** Setup wizard screen

**Script:**

"The setup page will ask you 5 questions.

Let me walk you through each one."

---

#### Question 1: Database

**Action:** Show the database screen

**Script:**

"Question 1 is about the database.

Do not worry about what this means.

Just click the button that says 'Test Connection.'

If you see a green checkmark, great.

Click Next."

---

#### Question 2: Admin Account

**Action:** Show the admin account form

**Script:**

"Question 2 is your admin account.

This is YOUR account.

You are the boss of your website.

Type your name.
Type your email.
Choose a password.

Write these down somewhere safe."

---

#### Question 3: Site Name

**Action:** Show the site name field

**Script:**

"Question 3 is your site name.

What do you want to call your website?

Maybe 'My Awesome Blog' or your business name.

Type it in."

---

#### Question 4: Plugins

**Action:** Show the plugin selection screen

**Script:**

"Question 4 is about features, or 'plugins.'

These add powers to your site.

SEO helps people find you on Google.
Comments let readers leave messages.
Security keeps hackers out.

Check the boxes for the ones you want.

You can always change this later."

---

#### Question 5: Theme

**Action:** Show the theme selection

**Script:**

"Question 5 is the fun one.

Pick a design for your site.

Click on one you like.

You can change it later too."

---

### [3:30] Step 6: Your Website Is Ready!

**On screen:** Dashboard appears

**Script:**

"Click Finish or Complete Setup.

And that is it!

Your website is now ready.

Welcome to your dashboard.

This is your control center.

From here you can write posts, upload photos, and more."

---

### [4:00] Creating Your First Blog Post

**On screen:** Dashboard → Posts → Add New

**Script:**

"Let me show you one more thing.

Let's publish your first blog post.

Click 'Posts' on the left menu."

**Action:** Click Posts

**Script:**

"Now click 'Add New.'"

**Action:** Click Add New

**Script:**

"Type a title. How about 'Hello World!'

Write a sentence or two in the big box.

When you are ready, click 'Publish' in the top-right corner.

Click 'Confirm.'

And boom! You just published your first post.

Your website now has content."

**Action:** Show the post on the public site

---

### [4:45] Where to Get Help

Here is where to get help:

Ask questions on GitHub Discussions.
Report bugs on GitHub Issues.

We are here to help you succeed."

**On screen:** Camera or closing screen with website URL

**Script:**

You built a website today.

That is amazing.

Go show it to your friends and family.

## 📝 Production Notes

### Audio Tips

- Speak slowly. Slower than you think.
- Pause between sentences.
- Smile while talking (it makes your voice friendly).
- Do a 30-second test recording to check your sound.

### Recording Tips

- Record in a quiet room.
- Close windows and doors.
- Turn off phone notifications.
- Use a microphone if you have one.

### Editing Tips

- Cut out long pauses.
- Speed up the installer running part.
- Add text captions for key steps.
- Add zoom effects on buttons the user should click.

### Optional Additions

- Add background music (low volume, instrumental)
- Add a progress bar overlay showing which step
- Add a final screen with links
